Getting Started with Windows 8.0
by Alice Carrole




Introduction

I have found Windows 8.0 exceptionally easy to use by spending a minimal amount of time viewing the Microsoft tutorials.  I have watched and listened to many people complain, where if they had spent even 25 minutes viewing a few videos and reading tutorials they would have not been so frustrated and would be finding their way around Windows 8 and most likely, enjoying their new experience. Below are the three that I would recommend, there are more available from Microsoft, and I will share that link in my summary.

Learning about the Start Menu

The start menu is different in Windows 8.0, it is now a Start Screen. The Start screen is the starting point for everything you do with your PC. You can think of it like the Start menu you’re used to, but now it’s full-screen, and you can personalize it with your apps, friends, and photos.
>> Learn more about the start screen from Microsoft.

Microsoft's video and text for this tutorial will take less than 10 minutes of your time.

Navigating Around in Windows 8.0

Learning  how to navigate on your PC, including how to get back to Start, switch between apps, and use apps side by side is really easy. Learn how to be more efficient with touch or a mouse and keyboard, and learn the top keyboard shortcuts.
>> Learn how to get around on your PC from Microsoft.

Microsoft's 3 videos and text for this tutorial will take less than 10 minutes of your time.

Personalizing the Start Menu

This is not essential, but I like to have my personal touch for my PC.   
>> Learn about personalizing your Start Menu from Microsoft.

Microsoft's videos and text for this tutorial will take less than 5 minutes of your time.

Summary

In less than 25 minutes you will be productively moving around and setting up your PC.   You will be happy with your invested time, less stress and frustration. In addition, you will be able to look at those techies who don't read the manual or watch a simple video and laugh as they spew their frustrations and ask YOU for help.
